RETAIL SALES SUFFER UNEXPECTED DROP - WORST FIGURES IN 9 YEARS

The numbers slumped for the month of December by 1.2 percent, closing out the holiday season on a weak note for retailers.

December retail sales saw their sharpest decline in more than nine years, suggesting slower economic growth amid government shutdown and the stock market turmoil.

There was a downward revision to data for November showing retail sales edged up by 0.1 percent instead of 0.2 percent for the month. Summing up 2018, sales were up 5 percent over 2017.

The drop surprised many economists because it happened in December, peak of the holiday season. Plummeting gas prices, volatile stock market and global tensions around trade were few things listed as causes underlying the negative numbers.

Amazon’s fourth quarter results exceeded expectations, recording a 20 percent increase in sales.

“Worries about trade are affecting businesses and consumer confidence,” said Jim O’Sullivan, chief U.S. economist of High Frequency Economics. “But why would it give such an exaggerated number is not clear.”

The nonstore retailers—which include e-commerce shopping—saw a shocking decline of 3.9 percent in sales, the biggest since November 2008.

The signs of bad online retail health came as a surprise due to its contrast with the sales figures reported by various retailers earlier.

The e-commerce giant, Amazon’s fourth quarter results exceeded expectations, recording a 20 percent increase in sales. Costco finished strong to this fiscal year and reported a 26 percent rise in its online sales.

While Andrew Zatlin, founder of SouthBay Research dismissed the numbers outright calling them a ‘result of a hasty job,’ Census Bureau rejected speculations affirming that the lapse in federal funding did not impact the accuracy of the report.

“Nonstore retail sales have increased almost every month since 2014,” said Zatlin. “The sudden drop this month makes no sense, there have to be mistakes in the data.”

Costco finished strong to this fiscal year and reported a 26 percent rise in its online sales.

While the shutdown raised credibility concerns around the retail numbers, it wasn’t a considerable headwind for the discouraging figures.

The shutdown began late in the month of December and since it wasn’t expected it couldn’t have had an enormous impact on the pre-holiday sales, says Ryan Sweet, head of monetary policy research at Moddy’s Analytics.

All major categories except motor vehicles and building materials posted a decline for the end of the year. The department stores sales recorded a 3.3 percent decline.

While decline in online retail sales remain unexplained, slump in offline merchandise sales did not raise any questions. Christmas this season has not been merry for Macy’s and Victoria’s Secret with both retailers previously reporting a downtick in sales during the holiday period.

The consumers however are not walking away. According to Sweet, the January sales will see an increase even though the numbers won’t slide back considerably.

No date for the January retail sales report has been set as of yet.
